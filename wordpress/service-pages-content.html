Powder Coating

Powder coating is our core trade. We apply a dry, electrostatically-charged powder to your metal and cure it in the oven to a tough, even film that far outlasts wet paint — resistant to chips, scratches, corrosion and UV.

We coat everything from production runs of brackets and fabrications to one-off personal projects like alloys, gates and furniture, in virtually any RAL colour and a choice of gloss, satin, matt and textured finishes.

Not sure which finish suits the job? Bring it in or send a photo and we'll talk you through the options.

Shot Blasting

Before colour goes anywhere near your parts they need to be clean. Our in-house shot blasting strips rust, mill scale, old paint and powder right back to bright, bare metal.

A properly blasted surface gives the coating a key to grip, so the finish bonds harder and lasts longer — the difference between a coat that looks good on day one and one that's still sound years later.

Because we blast and coat under one roof, your parts go from tired to finished in a single visit.

Pre-Treatment

Between blasting and coating we chemically pre-treat the metal to lock out corrosion. This stage cleans, degreases and lays down a conversion layer for the powder to bond to.

It's the unseen step that decides how a finish survives the weather and the years — particularly on architectural metalwork, fleet parts and anything that lives outdoors.

It adds a little time to the job and years to the finish, which is well worth it on anything that has to last.

Masking Service

Threads, machined faces, bearing bores and earth points often need to stay coating-free. We mask these areas by hand to your drawing or sample before the part goes into the booth.

Careful masking means your components fit, bolt up and conduct exactly as intended when they come back — no chasing powder out of threads on site.

Send a marked-up drawing or a sample and we'll match it exactly.
